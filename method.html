Method

Art is the expression of our human emotions. I chose photorealistic painting as a way of expressing a cheerful, happy view of the world around us. I use color, nostalgia items such as childrens' toys, dolls, and candy. Candy has been a common subject because it is easily recognizable, brings a smile to our faces, and makes us feel good.


Other features of my work include using cellophane wrapping to move the focus around the painting and give a realistic touch. The backgrounds are usually black to highlight the color of the subject even further. Norman Rockwell used white as a background in many of his works the way I use black.


In summary, the paintings use familiar objects and color is important. Contrast is key to highlight the objects. In my recent work I use many different objects to move the eye around the work. I always use photography in setting up the subject. Enjoy!
